Learning Plan Blog Post

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

  • Anything in the tech world honestly. I love everything that involves tech and just want to be part of it. I'm looking forward to the end of this course when I'll get help to find a job with Dev Academy, or maybe I'll get one with my father's company.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning.

  • Throughout Foundations, I learned about myself more: Problem-solving is still my strong suit, but I now realize that I might be too independent to try to work with others. I learned from self-assessment that I need to improve my ability to communicate, especially in team projects. This shall give me a starting point to turn myself into a better team player for Bootcamp.

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

  • I would like to improve in communication and teamwork skills: to feel comfortable working with others around me, giving and receiving constructive feedback, and being able to contribute to the group effectively. Also, I want to increase my level of self-awareness and reflective skills so that I'll be able to understand where I can improve continuously in all aspects.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

  • I will do this by breaking down tasks into manageable pieces, setting priorities clearly, and maintaining a balanced schedule that includes breaks to avoid burnout. I will communicate openly with others, respect differences in working style, and ensure fairness in my contribution in group settings.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

  • Whenever I am struggling with something, I know I can always send a message in the Discord channel asking for support in whatever it is I am doing. Though, I will only be asking for help if I'm stuck on something for an extended amount of time.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team.

  • The only things I expect from the facilitation team are just to help me through this course and be nice, I guess.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things.

  • Currently nothing is in the way of me completing course work, except for things that might just pop out that I have to do.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

  • My biggest strengths I think is my persistence and problem-solving skills. I am good at breaking down complex problems and finding solutions. My limitations might include time management and asking for help when needed. I sometimes try to figure things out on my own for too long before seeking assistance.

Based on your knowledge of Bootcamp, how and where will you seek help in a timely way?

  • I will seek help from my peers, facilitators, and available resources like documentation or google. I plan to ask questions as soon as I recognize a problem I can’t solve on my own, rather than letting it hold back my progress.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

  • I will be committed, focused, and open to learning. I expect to push myself beyond what feels comfortable with regard to communication and teamwork.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

  • I expect the Bootcamp team to provide guidance and support when needed, but also to challenge me to think critically and solve problems independently.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

  • My biggest non-technical challenge will likely be staying focused and managing stress.
